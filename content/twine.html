Twine Stuff
Once upon a time, I did some Twine work that was useful to people, or was interesting to me, and in rare cases, maybe even both.
Entweedle
Story Format Source Assets
Those of us who used Twine 1.x and exported stories in Twee format as part of a larger workflow were disappointed to find that Twine 2.0 no longer had that feature. I created Entweedle as a One Game a Month project for my own use and for anyone else who needed it. Playing a story with Entweedle set as its story format will display Twee format source code for your story.
However, this capability was eventually added to Twine 2.x, so this story format is probably obsolete. It's maintained here in case there's any other use for it.
Illume
Story Format Source Assets
More of an experiment, Illume is a proofing story format for collaborators. It generates a file which can be saved and passed off to a reviewer or proofreader. This "Illumation" of the story keeps track of which passages have been reviewed, and allows the reader to make changes to any passage. When finished, the reader can export a document that details all of proposed changes, which the author in turn can go through like a checklist to revise their story.
While this generally works as intended, the process is still inconvenient due to the limitations of being a story format, since there's no direct way of incorporating the changes back into the original story. Anything more graceful, however, would probably require a version of Twine with shared, server-stored stories and multiple users, which is obviously a very large and fundamental change to how Twine works, so I wouldn't hold my breath waiting for that.
A Penitent Man
Launch Story
While participating in two creative challenges, NaNoWriMo and One Game a Month, I had the ill-advised notion that I would combine the two challenges by, in the span of one month, writing a new story of at least 50,000 words for NaNoWriMo that was playable as an interactive fiction story for 1GAM. The result is "A Penitent Man," an interactive interview between a medium and a dead man.
In light of the time constraints, the story received essentially no proofreading, no editing, and no corrections. At the time I jokingly described this story as "naked pictures of my brain on the internet" and that still feels accurate. I don't dare break the seal now, so these decade-old typos and clumsy phrasings will just have to do.
Night Crossing
Launch Story
In the midst of my gamejamming era, I came across a jam on the then-official Twine forums where the goal was to create a Twine story in 10 days having less than 1,000 total words. This time I experimented with embedding a Twine story in an existing web page. I figured the least Twine-like thing I could do is incorporate some sort of active elements, so I put together a page with a layered scrolling view out a window and some background sound, embedded the story format code, and plugged the updated Twine story elements into the page whenever I exported it.
The combination of an existing page and a Twine export worked as intended, so that was a win. The story itself has a looping structure to make the most of its limited permitted word count, and while it's a little barebones, I think it at least managed to achieve a certain vibe.
